Introducing Easy Docs

Custom built content types will populate your site as you create your content.

* Theme cost is valid for one site and includes 4 hrs of my time to help customize the site to your brand.

Documentation Home Page

All your documentation is collected on your home page so your end users can easily find the information that they are looking for.

This page will populate it's self as you add FAQ, Guides and News. These are all Barley custom content types so they couldn't be easier to edit.

FAQ Made Easy

Frequently asked questions are always an important part of a documentation site. You can add each question and answer with the custom content type for FAQs.

Guides

Guides will be the heart and sole of your documentation site. Each guide has a title, subtitle and content area. The content area can be what ever you make it. The theme is formated to display your copy, images, code and videos so you can focus on the content.

Your guides will be automatically added to your Guides page and in the Guides section on the home page.

News

Hopefully more often than not you'll have exciting and/or important news to share with your end users. You'll be able to do this easily and in style with the News content type.

Adding your Branding is... well it's Easy

Adding your branding colors and logo couldn't be much easier. With the use of a LESS CSS variables file and the Barley logo image data attribute you can have your site branded in minutes.

Responsive

Your documentation site is also fully responsive and will work in all devices. So if you end users are mobile and need to reference you documentation you can rest easy knowing it will look great on their mobile device.

Built with Bootstrap, Less and of course Barley.

Barley Themer themes are built with Bootstrap(v2) and less so it should be easy to update and customize.

Less files are included with the download. All customization was done in one custom.less file and you can change all your colors in the variables.less file.

Because all changes are done in the custom and variables file, you can still update bootstrap if you need/want to.
